# PROBABILITY & STATISTICS

## COMBINATORICS

*   **Combination:** $N = nCr = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} \rightarrow nCr$
*   **Permutation:** $N = nPr = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$
*   **Circular Permutation:**
    *   Flip: $N = (n-1)! / 2$
    *   Non-flip: $N = (n-1)!$
*   **Alternation:** $N = g! \times (a!)^g$ (This formula seems unusual/specific; usually, alternation depends on the total arrangement of two groups.)
*   **Proximity:** $N = (n+1)(n! \times p!)$ (This formula also seems unusual/specific.)

## Standard Poker Playing Cards
*   A standard deck has 52 cards.
*   **Suits:** Clubs (♣), Diamonds (♦), Hearts (♥), Spades (♠)
*   **Ranks:** Ace, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack, Queen, King
*   Each suit has 13 cards.
*   **Face cards:** Jack, Queen, King (3 per suit, 12 total)

## LAWS OF PROBABILITY

*   **General Addition Rule:** $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) – P(A \cap B)$
*   **Mutually Exclusive Events:** $P(A \cap B) = 0$
*   **Binomial Distribution (n < 20):** $P(X=x) = nC_x \times p^x \times (1-p)^{n-x}$
*   **Poisson Distribution (n > 20):** $P(X=x) = \frac{e^{-np} (np)^x}{x!}$
*   **Bayesian Probability (Bayes' Theorem):** $P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \times P(A)}{P(B)}$

| :----------- | :------------------------------------ | :---------------- | :-------------------- | :---- |

## Central Tendency

*   **Arithmetic mean ($\bar{x}$):** $\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n} = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}$
*   **Root mean square (RMS):** $\sqrt{(\sum x^2 / n)}$
*   **Geometric mean:** $(x_1 x_2 \dots x_n)^{1/n}$
*   **Harmonic mean:** $n \div \sum (1/x)$

---

## KURTOSIS and SKEWNESS

### KURTOSIS
*   **Leptokurtic:** High peak, heavy tails.
*   **Mesokurtic:** Normal distribution (e.g., bell curve).
*   **Platykurtic:** Flat peak, light tails.

### SKEWNESS
*   **Positive Skew:** Mean > Median (tail to the right).
*   **Symmetrical Distribution:** Mean = Median = Mode.
*   **Negative Skew:** Mean < Median (tail to the left).

## NORMAL DISTRIBUTION (Gaussian Distribution)
A bell-shaped curve used to model real-world processes and phenomena.

### Normal Distribution Case 1: (Given x, s, and $\bar{x}$)
*   For $<x: P((x-\bar{x})/s)$
*   For $>x: R((x-\bar{x})/s)$

### Normal Distribution Case 2: (Given table data)
*   For $<x: P(x>t)$ (This notation implies using a z-table for probabilities, where 't' might be a z-score)
*   For $>x: R(x>t)$ (This notation implies using a z-table for probabilities)
